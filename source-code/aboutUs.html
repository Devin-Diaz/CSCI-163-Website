Our Motivation & Approach Toward the Research on AI in CS Education Today

My name is Tyler Reed and I am one of the contributors to this research project. I am a senior and majoring in Computer Science at FSU. My motivation was already being a computer science student and reflecting on how computer science was taught to me before AI as we know it today became widely available. Additionally, since I am a senior student I have observed the potenial that AI can offer for my future career, and learning style.


However, I am aware of the drawbacks that come with relying too heavily on AI in my learning process. My approach was to capture the use of AI in computer science classrooms and how it is being used by students. I then gathered 3 articles about AI in CS education and compiled my research findings to have students gain a better understanding of the impact of AI on CS education, student learning, and career prospects.

My name is Devin Diaz and I am a Computer Science student with a strong interest in how technology shapes both learning and professional development. Throughout my academic journey, I have explored various areas including software engineering and data engineering, while also building projects that reflect real-world applications.


This research topic is particularly relevant to me because I have experienced both sides of AI in education using it as a powerful tool to learn and improve, while also recognizing how easily it can replace genuine understanding if used incorrectly. My goal with this project is to highlight that balance and emphasize the importance of developing real skills alongside AI rather than depending on it.
